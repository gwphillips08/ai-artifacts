Strategy Overview

The strategy is a variation of the thematic approach that attempts to capture several durable investment themes by investing in select companies that exhibit thematic purity and a proven track record of using their knowledge base and competitive advantages to adapt to new technologies and changing customer needs.

While this suggests a focus on quality-growth exposure, we want to remain open-minded. The portfolio will always include at least 5 Starter and Tryout positions that will explore narrower and less established areas of technological advancement.

Defining themes is an important starting point. We do not like to define themes too broadly, like AI, aging populations, renewable energy, water shortage or sustainability. This would defeat thematic purity and draw in too much noise. Similarly, we do not want to chase headlines with one-trick ponies or engage in short-term stock picking.

Rapid technological change means that the future of firms can look very different from their past. Fundamentally, this approach is neither about extrapolation nor mean reversion, both of which will likely prove less effective than in previous regimes, it is about evolution and revenue growth that results in increasing intrinsic value. This inevitably involves making a judgement about how successful the management team will be in executing its strategy.

What We Are Looking For

  • Technological leadership in a particular sector or category
  • Value-enhancing revenue growth. Strong gross profit margins, recurring and loyal customers, high moats. Free cash flow is an important, but secondary consideration. This helps avoid "cost-cutters" while being open to early-stage companies that may be currently burning cash.
  • AI resilient does not equal AI immune. High switching costs buy time, not permanence. AI doesn't need to replace these products outright, it will just gradually hollow out incremental growth by collapsing seats, workflows, or interfaces.
  • Annual EPS growth, solid balance sheet – core positions should show meaningful growth for the last 3 years.
  • New things — companies with new products, new management, or significant new changes in industry conditions.

Portfolio Structure

Concentrated, around 20 positions in 4 sleeves:

Core High Conviction Positions
weight: 5–10% each
Up to 12 positions · holding period 6 months to several years
Starter Positions
weight: 2–3% each
Up to 3 positions · holding period up to a year
Initial entries to potentially graduate to Core
Speculative Positions
weight: ~1% each
1–4 positions · holding period up to a year
Attractive risk-reward based on specific opportunities
Cash Sleeve
~10%
Around 10% as a cushion to capitalize on market volatility and opportunities

Stop-Loss

15–20% drop

Structural trends often experience sharp, temporary momentum swings; our stop-loss strategy will focus on the specific behavior of the theme or a given stock. While it will not always trigger a sale, a 15–20% drop in any stock will trigger an evaluation of company specific events or missteps that may have caused it.
