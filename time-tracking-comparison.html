Notes & Considerations
Toggl Track

Best UX & reporting for agencies. Profitability tracking requires Premium ($18/user). No native expense tracking or PTO — you'll need add-ons or Harvest for those. QB integration is one-way (sends invoices to QB, not time data directly). Salesforce integration is a differentiator vs competitors.

Harvest ⚠

Acquired by Bending Spoons in 2025. Users are reporting major price increases at renewal — some 10–100x prior rates. The smoothest invoicing workflow in the category. Forecast (resource scheduling) is a separate paid product at +$9/user/mo. Evaluate carefully given acquisition risk.

Clockify

Best value at scale — ended unlimited free plan in April 2026 (now capped at 5 users). QB integration available at Standard tier ($5.49/user/mo). Utilitarian UI but full-featured. No payroll automation. Strong expense tracking and time-off on Pro+. Good middle-ground for growing teams.

Hubstaff

Most features overall, especially for remote/field teams. Only platform with payroll automation, GPS, and QB Desktop support. QB integration auto-syncs hourly (Online) or via .IIF export (Desktop). Requires QB integration on Grow plan ($7.50/user/mo); unlimited integrations on Team ($10). 2-seat minimum on all paid plans.

QuickBooks Integration Summary

All four platforms integrate with QuickBooks Online. Harvest and Hubstaff offer the deepest integrations (bi-directional / auto-sync). Hubstaff is the only option supporting QB Desktop. Clockify offers the most affordable QB-enabled plan at $5.49/user/mo. Toggl's QB sync is invoice-focused only.

Best Fit Guide

Toggl → Agencies valuing UI & reporting. Harvest → Freelancers who bill by the hour (but watch pricing). Clockify → Budget-conscious teams needing solid QB integration. Hubstaff → Remote/field teams needing monitoring, payroll & QB sync.
